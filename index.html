Mechanical Design and CAD

Mechanical engineers use advanced computer-aided design (CAD) software to create high-resolution 3D models and precise blueprints for everything from small medical tools to massive skyscrapers.This process allows for "digital intent" to be translated into actual fabricated components, ensuring that parts fit together with extremely tight tolerances before a single piece of material is cut.Beyond just drawing, design is a capstone experience that integrates theory with hardware building to solve real-world problems for customers and industries.

Materials Science and Solid Mechanics

Selecting the right materials involves evaluating metals, plastics, and new composites to ensure they can withstand environmental stressors like corrosion, extreme heat, or high pressure.Engineers must analyze how these materials are sourced and manufactured, often setting strict targets for strength and durability while also considering sustainability and emissions.Advanced research even looks at the "circular economy," focusing on materials that are non-toxic, non-flammable, and easy to recycle at the end of a product's life.

Thermodynamics and Heat Transfer

This pillar focuses on managing heat and power efficiently, such as designing radiators and cooling systems to handle the massive waste heat produced by combustion engines or industrial lasers.A major goal in modern engineering is improving energy efficiency to create more sustainable transportation systems and reduce the environmental impact of power generation.Engineers also work on thermal management for infrastructure, ensuring that buildings and machines maintain safe temperatures regardless of the external climate.

Fluid Mechanics

Fluid mechanics is essential for controlling the flow of gases and liquids, which is used to design complex filtering systems for industrial smoke or the high-pressure water jets used in precision cutting.In the aerospace and automotive industries, fluid dynamics are used to shape vehicles so they glide through the air with minimal drag, which helps meet strict fuel economy and greenhouse gas standards.Understanding fluid behavior also allows engineers to design efficient pumps and valves that are used in everything from household plumbing to the fuel systems of rocket engines.
